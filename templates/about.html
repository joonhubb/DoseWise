ABOUT DOSEWISE

Why vaccines matter — and why we built this

⚠️ The Crisis No One Talks About

Every year, over 1.5 million children die from diseases that vaccines could have prevented. Not because the vaccines don't exist — but because families missed doses, lost track of schedules, or simply didn't know what their child needed.

In low-income and rural communities, parents often have no access to clear, personalized vaccination guidance. A missed dose at 6 months can mean a child is vulnerable to polio, diphtheria, or whooping cough for years.

🦠 How Vaccines Changed History

Smallpox once killed 300 million people in the 20th century alone. Today it is completely eradicated — the only human disease ever wiped off the face of the earth — entirely because of vaccines.

Polio paralyzed hundreds of thousands of children every year in the 1950s. Thanks to the oral polio vaccine, cases have dropped by over 99.9% globally. We are on the verge of eradicating it entirely.

The COVID-19 pandemic reminded the world just how devastating infectious disease can be — and how transformative vaccines are when deployed quickly and equitably.

💉 What Happens When You Miss a Dose

Vaccines work through a series of timed doses — each one building on the last. Missing even one dose can leave a child partially protected or completely unprotected against diseases like measles, hepatitis B, and tetanus.

The WHO estimates that vaccine-preventable diseases cause 25% of all deaths in children under 5 globally. Most of these deaths happen not because vaccines are unavailable — but because caregivers don't know their child is overdue.

🌍 Why We Built DoseWise

DoseWise was built for the GNEC Hackathon 2026 in response to SDG 3 — Good Health and Well-being. Our goal is simple: make WHO-standard vaccination guidance accessible to every parent, everywhere, for free.

By entering a child's age and vaccination history, parents instantly see which vaccines are overdue, which are coming up, and what they need to do next. No medical training required. No expensive consultations. Just clear, actionable guidance.

Health inequality is one of the defining issues of our time. DoseWise is our small contribution to closing that gap — one child at a time.
