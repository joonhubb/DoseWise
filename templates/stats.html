📊 The Vaccination Gap

Despite vaccines being one of the most cost-effective health interventions ever developed, millions of children remain unvaccinated or under-vaccinated every year. The gap is largest in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia, where healthcare infrastructure is limited and families often lack access to clear guidance.

The WHO's Expanded Programme on Immunization (EPI) has saved an estimated 154 million lives since 1974 — making it one of the greatest public health achievements in history. Yet we still lose 1.5 million children annually to diseases we have vaccines for.

🚨 The Misinformation Threat

Vaccine hesitancy — driven by misinformation on social media — has been listed by the WHO as one of the top 10 threats to global health. Measles cases have surged in countries that previously had near-zero rates, entirely due to declining vaccination coverage.

DoseWise fights this by providing clear, WHO-based, science-backed guidance — making it easier for parents to do the right thing for their children.

🎯 SDG 3 — Good Health & Well-being

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 3 calls for ensuring healthy lives and promoting well-being for all at all ages. Target 3.b specifically calls for supporting access to vaccines for all countries, particularly developing nations.

DoseWise directly supports SDG 3.b by making WHO vaccination schedules accessible to any parent with internet access — breaking down barriers of language, literacy, and geography.
